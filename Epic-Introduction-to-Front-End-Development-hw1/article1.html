Great poet Homer

Homer was an ancient Greek poet who is believed to have lived around the 8th century BCE. He is credited with writing two of the most famous and influential epic poems of all time - the Iliad and the Odyssey. The Iliad tells the story of the Trojan War, focusing on the final weeks of a 10-year siege of the city of Troy. It is considered a foundational work of Western literature. The Odyssey follows the journey of the Greek hero Odysseus as he tries to make his way home after the Trojan War. It is an adventure epic that has had a massive impact on subsequent literature. Homer's exact dates are uncertain, but most scholars agree he lived in the 8th century BCE, making him one of the earliest known Greek writers. His poems were originally passed down orally for generations before being written down, likely in the 6th century BCE. Homer's works used a unique poetic form called dactylic hexameter, which became the standard for Greek epic poetry. This metrical style contributes to the elevated, majestic quality of his language. Both the Iliad and Odyssey were hugely influential on Greek and Roman literature, as well as the entire Western literary tradition. They remain foundational texts that are still widely studied today.
